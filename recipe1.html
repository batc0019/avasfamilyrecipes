Apple Pie with Caramel Sauce


story

This recipe is probably the reason why I developed a love for pie at a young age. I was never a fan of birthday cake as a kid, so every year there was a request for "birthday pie"--and it was usually this one. I hope you enjoy it as much as I did as a kid, and still do.

Original recipe: Waverman, L. (2002, Oct 05). A pair of perfect pies for fall. The Globe and Mail.



Part 1: No Fail Flaky Pastry


ingredients

Ingredients list
3 cups all purpose flour
1 tbsp granulated sugar (optional)
1 tsp salt
1/2 cup unsalted butter, cut in 8 pieces
1/2 cup vegetable shortening, cut in 8 pieces
1/3 cup cold water
1 tbsp white vinegar or lemon juice

tools
  • pastry blender
  • knife
  • rolling pin
  • bowls
  • plastic wrap

instructions
  1. In a bowl, combine flour, sugar and salt. With a pastry blender, or using fingers, cut in butter and vegetable shortening until mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  2. Combine water and vinegar and sprinkle over flour mixture a little at a time, tossing together with a fork until the pastry is moist enough to hold its shape when a little piece is pressed between fingers.
  3. Gather pastry into a ball. It should not feel sticky. Divide into 2 portions, cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 2 hours or overnight.
  4. Roll out each portion as needed. Makes enough pastry for two 9-inch single crust pies or one double.

Part 2: Apple Pie


ingredients

Ingredients list
1 recipe No Fail Flaky Pastry
3 lbs Spy apples or Granny Smith or Golden Delicious
1/4 cup granulated sugar
1/2 cup brown sugar
3 tbsp all-purpose flour
pinch salt
1/4 tsp nutmeg
1 tsp cinnamon
1 tsp grated lemon zest
1 tsp grated orange rind
1 tbsp orange juice
2 tbsp butter

tools
  • pastry blender
  • fork
  • rolling pin
  • bowls
  • knife
  • peeler
  • cutting board
  • 9" pie plate

instructions
  1. Preheat the oven to 425 F (220 C).
  2. On a floured board, roll out half the pastry and fit into a 9-inch (23 cm) pie plate or flan pan with removable base. Leave a 1/2 inch (1.25 cm) overhang.
  3. Peel, core and thinly slice apples. In a large bowl, combine the sugars, flour, salt, nutmeg, cinnamon and grated lemon and orange rind. Toss the apples in mixture. Sprinkle with orange juice. Pile into pie shell, mounding the filling high in the centre. Dot with butter.
  4. Roll out the remaining pastry and fit over the apples. Trim and crimp the edges together. Make two vents in the centre of pie to allow steam to escape.
  5. Bake for 10 minutes. Lower the heat to 375 F (190 C) and bake for a further 45 to 50 minutes, or until the pie crust turns golden, the apples are tender and filling is bubbling. Makes 8 servings.

Part 3: Apple Caramel Sauce


ingredients

Ingredients list
3/4 cup apple cider
1/2 cup granulated sugar
pinch cinnamon

tools
  • saucepan
  • whisk
  • spatula

instructions
  1. In a saucepan, combine 1/2 cup (125 mL) apple cider and sugar. Bring to a boil on high heat and boil until mixture turns a golden colour.
  2. Remove from heat and quickly pour in remaining cider along with cinnamon. It will spurt a little. Stir until incorporated into sauce.
  3. Serve the sauce hot, warm or cold. The sauce thickens when cold. Makes 1 cup.
